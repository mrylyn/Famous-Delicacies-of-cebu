STEAMED DUMPINGS(DINUGUAN BUN):



Ingredients



For the Dumpling Wrapper:


  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt


    • For the Dinuguan Filling


  • 1 cup Dinuguan (Filipino blood stew)
  • 1 cup ground pork
  • 1/2 cup chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste


    • Instructions



  • Prepare the Dumpling Wrapper:

    a. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, warm water, and salt.

    b. Knead the mixture until a smooth dough is formed. c. Cover the dough and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

  • Make the Dinuguan Filling:

    a. In a pan, heat cooking oil over medium heat.

    b. Sauté chopped onions until translucent.

    c. Add ground pork and cook until browned.
    d. Pour in Dinuguan and soy sauce. Mix well.
    e. Simmer until the mixture thickens. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
    f. Allow the Dinuguan filling to cool.

  • Assemble the Steamed Dumplings:

    a. Roll out the rested dough on a floured surface into a thin sheet.

    b. Cut the dough into small circles using a round cutter.

    c. Spoon a small amount of the Dinuguan filling onto the center of each dough circle.

    d. Fold the dough in half, creating a half-moon shape. Pinch the edges to seal the dumpling.

    e. Place the dumplings on a steamer lined with parchment paper.

  • Steam the Dumplings:

    a. Steam the dumplings for about 15-20 minutes or until the wrapper is cooked.

    b. Check regularly to prevent overcooking and ensure the dumplings are thoroughly steamed.

  • Serve:

    a. Once done, carefully remove the dumplings from the steamer.

    b. Arrange on a serving plate and serve hot.


    • SHRIMP DUMPLINGS (HAKAW):



    Ingredients



  • 1 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/4 cup water chestnuts, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons spring onions, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1 pack round dumpling wrappers


    • Dipping Sauce:



  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger, finely grated


    • Instructions



  • Prepare the Filling:

    a. In a food processor, combine shrimp, water chestnuts, spring onions, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and white pepper.

    b. Pulse until the mixture becomes a coarse paste. Do not over-process; you want some texture in the filling.

  • Assemble the Dumplings:

    a. Take a dumpling wrapper and place a teaspoon of the shrimp mixture in the center.

    b. Moisten the edges of the wrapper with water, then fold it in half, creating a half-moon shape.

    c. Pinch the edges to seal, ensuring there are no air pockets. Repeat for the remaining wrappers.

  • Steam the Dumplings:

    a. Arrange the dumplings on a lined steamer basket, ensuring they are not touching each other.

    b. Steam over high heat for about 8-10 minutes or until the dumplings are translucent and the filling is cooked through.

  • Prepare the Dipping Sauce:

    a. In a small bowl, mix together soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, and grated ginger until well combined.

    b. Transfer the steamed shrimp dumplings to a serving plate.

    Serve immediately with the prepared dipping sauce.


    • Pork Dumplings (Siopao):



    Ingredients



    For the Dough:


  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon shortening or cooking oil


    • For the Filling:


  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup oyster sauce
  • 1/4 cup hoisin sauce
  • 1 cup chopped water chestnuts
  • 1 cup chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • Salt and pepper to taste


    • Instructions



  • For the Dough:

    a. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, and baking powder.

    b. Gradually add warm water and mix until a dough forms.

    c. Knead the dough on a floured surface until smooth.

    d. Rub the dough with shortening or oil, cover, and let it rest for at least 1 hour.

  • For the Filling:

    a. In a large bowl, mix together ground pork, soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin sauce, sugar, and cornstarch.

    b. Stir in water chestnuts, green onions, sesame oil, salt, and pepper.

    c. Refrigerate the filling for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  • Assembly:

    a. Divide the dough into small portions and roll each into a ball.

    b. Flatten each ball into a round wrapper, ensuring the edges are thinner than the center.

    c. Spoon a portion of the filling into the center of the wrapper.

    d. Pinch the edges together to seal and create a pleated pattern on top.

    e. Place each siopao on a square of parchment paper.

  • Steaming:

    a. Arrange the siopao in a steamer basket, leaving space between each.

    b. Steam for 15-20 minutes or until the dough is cooked and the filling is hot.

    c. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious Cebu-style Pork Dumplings!


    • Bean Curd Rolls (Tofu Rolls):



    Ingredients



  • 1 pack of firm tofu
  • 1 cup julienned carrots
  • 1 cup julienned bell peppers (assorted colors)
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/2 cup julienned cucumber
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Spring roll wrappers
  • Cooking oil for frying


    • Instructions



  • Drain the tofu and pat it dry with paper towels.

  • Cut the tofu into rectangular strips, about 3 inches long and 1/2 inch wide.
    Season the tofu with a pinch of salt and set aside.

  • In a bowl, combine julienned carrots, bell peppers, bean sprouts, cucumber, and chopped cilantro.

  • In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, grated ginger, and minced garlic.

  • Pour the dressing over the vegetable mixture and toss until well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  • Take a spring roll wrapper and dip it in warm water for a few seconds until it softens.

  • Lay the wrapper on a flat surface and place a strip of tofu in the center.

  • Spoon a generous amount of the vegetable filling on top of the tofu strip.

  • Fold the sides of the wrapper over the filling and then roll it up tightly, sealing the edge with a bit of water.

  • Repeat the process with the remaining tofu strips and filling.

  • Heat cooking oil in a pan over medium heat.

  • Fry the tofu rolls until they turn golden brown and crispy on all sides.

  • Remove from the oil and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil.

  • Allow the bean curd rolls to cool slightly before serving.

  • Serve with your favorite dipping sauce, such as sweet chili sauce or soy sauce with a dash of lime juice.


    • Fried Dumplings (Pork and Shrimp Guisado):



    Ingredients



  • 1 cup ground pork
  • 1/2 cup minced shrimp
  • 1/4 cup minced water chestnuts
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • Dumpling wrappers
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1/4 cup water (for steaming)


    • Instructions



  • In a bowl, combine ground pork, minced shrimp, minced water chestnuts, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, sugar, and ground black pepper.

  • Mix the ingredients thoroughly until well combined.

  • Take a dumpling wrapper and place a teaspoon of the filling in the center.

  • Moisten the edges of the wrapper with water.

  • Fold the wrapper in half, sealing the edges to form a half-moon shape.

  • Pinch the edges to create pleats and ensure a tight seal.

  • Arrange the dumplings in a steamer lined with parchment paper.

  • Steam the dumplings for about 15-20 minutes or until they are cooked through.

  • Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium-high heat.

  • Place the steamed dumplings in the pan and fry until the bottoms turn golden brown.

  • Remove the dumplings from the pan and place them on a serving plate.

  • Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce, such as soy sauce mixed with calamansi or vinegar with minced garlic.


    • Vegetable Dumpling(Vegetarian Siomai):



    Ingredients



  • 2 cups finely chopped cabbage
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 cup chopped mushrooms
  • 1 cup tofu, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup chopped water chestnuts
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (vegetarian substitute if desired)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 package siomai/spring roll wrappers
  • Soy sauce, calamansi (or lemon), and sesame oil for dipping sauce


    • Instructions



  • In a large mixing bowl, combine chopped cabbage, shredded carrots, chopped mushrooms, crumbled tofu, water chestnuts, and green onions.

  • Add minced garlic, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, cornstarch, and ground black pepper to the bowl.
    Mix the ingredients thoroughly, ensuring an even distribution of flavors.

  • Lay out a siomai/spring roll wrapper on a clean surface.

  • Place a spoonful of the vegetable filling in the center of the wrapper.

  • Fold the wrapper in half over the filling, creating a semi-circle.

  • Pinch the edges together, creating pleats along the seam, ensuring the siomai is securely sealed.

  • Arrange the assembled siomai on a steamer lined with parchment paper.

  • Steam for approximately 15-20 minutes or until the siomai is cooked through.

  • While steaming, mix soy sauce, calamansi (or lemon) juice, and a few drops of sesame oil to create a dipping sauce. Adjust proportions to taste.

  • Once cooked, remove the siomai from the steamer and let them cool slightly.

  • Serve the vegetarian siomai with the dipping sauce.




    • DRIED FRUIT:



    Ingredients



  • 2 cups ripe fruit of your choice, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup water


    • Instructions



  • Peel and thinly slice them into uniform pieces.

  • In a saucepan, combine 1 cup of water and 1 cup of granulated sugar.

  • Stir over medium heat until the sugar completely dissolves, creating a simple syrup.

  • Place the sliced fruits in a bowl.

  • Pour the prepared sugar syrup over the fruits slices, ensuring they are fully submerged.

  • Let the fruits slices soak in the syrup for about 30 minutes to allow them to absorb the sweetness.

  • After soaking, drain the fruits slices, removing excess syrup.

  • Arrange the fruits slices on a wire rack or a lined baking sheet, ensuring they are spaced apart.

  • Place the arranged fruits slices in direct sunlight.

  • Allow them to dry for 2 to 3 days, turning the slices occasionally for even drying.

  • Bring the fruits indoors during the night to prevent moisture absorption.

  • If you have a food dehydrator, place the fruits slices on the trays.

  • Follow the dehydrator's instructions for drying fruit, typically around 135°F (57°C) for 8-12 hours.

  • The dried fruits should be leathery but slightly pliable. They should not be sticky.

  • Allow the dried fruits slices to cool completely.

  • Store them in an airtight container or sealable bags to maintain freshness.


    • OTAP:



    Ingredients



  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup margarine, softened
  • 1/4 cup evaporated milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon anise seeds (optional)
  • Additional flour for dusting


    • For the Sugar Glaze:

  • 1 pack round dumpling wrappers
  • 1/4 cup water


    • Instructions



  • In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, sugar, and salt.

  • Add the softened margarine and mix until the mixture becomes crumbly.
    Incorporate the evaporated milk and water gradually while continuously mixing.

  • Add anise seeds if desired and knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  • Divide the dough into small portions and roll each portion into a thin sheet on a floured surface.

  • Fold the sheet in half lengthwise, and then roll it tightly from one end to the other to form a cylindrical shape.

  • Cut the cylinder into smaller pieces, each about 2 inches in length.

  • Take each cut piece and flatten it with a rolling pin to achieve a thin oval shape. The thinner, the better.

  • Create a pattern on the surface by lightly pressing a fork or a toothpick. This adds the classic texture to the otap.

  • Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).

  • Arrange the shaped otap pieces on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  • Bake for about 15-20 minutes or until the edges turn golden brown.

  • Allow the otap to cool completely.

  • In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Bring to a simmer until the sugar dissolves and the mixture thickens slightly.

  • Brush the sugar glaze over the cooled otap pieces, coating them evenly.

  • Once the sugar glaze sets, your otap is ready to be enjoyed! Serve with your favorite hot beverage.


    • Deep-Fried Wanton (Dinuguan Chicharon):



    Ingredients



  • 1 cup Dinuguan (Pork Blood Stew)
  • Wonton wrappers
  • Cooking oil for deep-frying
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Green onions, chopped (for garnish)


    • Instructions



  • Prepare Dinuguan:

    - If you don't have Dinuguan ready, cook a batch using your preferred recipe.
    Dinuguan is a savory Filipino stew made with pork, often including offal, in a flavorful mixture of pig blood, garlic, chili, and vinegar.

  • Assemble Wantons:

    - Lay out wonton wrappers and spoon a small amount of Dinuguan onto the center of each wrapper.

  • Seal and Shape:

    - Moisten wrapper edges with water. Fold in half to create a triangle, pressing edges to seal.
    Optionally, shape them into small squares or other creative forms.

  • Heat Oil:

    - In a deep pan or fryer, heat cooking oil over medium-high heat.
    Ensure there's enough oil to submerge the wantons.

  • Deep-Fry Wantons:

    - Carefully add wantons to hot oil in batches. Fry until golden brown and crispy, around 2-3 minutes per side.

  • Drain and Season:

    - Use a slotted spoon to remove deep-fried wantons, placing them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
    Season with salt and pepper while hot.

  • Serve:

    - Arrange Dinuguan Chicharon on a serving plate. Garnish with chopped green onions for freshness and color.


    • Scallion Pancake (Kuchay Ah):



    Ingredients



  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup hot water
  • 1 cup chopped scallions (green onions)
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste


    • Instructions



  • Prepare the Dough:

    a. In a large mixing bowl, gradually add hot water to the all-purpose flour.

    b. Stir the mixture with a wooden spoon until it forms a dough.

    c. Knead the dough on a floured surface until it becomes smooth.

    d. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

  • Divide and Roll:

    a. Divide the dough into four equal portions.

    b. Take one portion and roll it out on a floured surface into a thin, large circle.

  • Add Scallions and Sesame Oil:

    a. Spread a thin layer of sesame oil over the rolled-out dough.

    b. Sprinkle a generous amount of chopped scallions evenly over the dough.

  • Roll and Coil:

    a. Roll the dough with scallions into a log.

    b. Coil the log into a round dough ball.

  • Repeat:

    - Repeat the process for the remaining dough portions.

  • Flatten and Roll Again:

    - Take one coiled dough ball and flatten it into a thin pancake on a floured surface.

  • Cooking:

    a. Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.

    b. Carefully place the flattened pancake into the hot oil.

    c. Cook until the pancake becomes golden brown and crispy on both sides.

  • Serve:

    a. Remove the pancake from the pan and place it on a paper towel to absorb excess oil.

    b. Slice the pancake into wedges and serve it hot.

    • BUKAYO:



    Ingredients



  • 4 cups grated mature coconut
  • 2 cups brown sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)


    • Instructions



  • Use mature coconut for a richer flavor. Peel the brown skin off the coconut and grate it finely.

  • In a large pan, combine the grated coconut and water.
    Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
    Continue cooking until the coconut becomes slightly toasted and aromatic.
    This step usually takes around 10-15 minutes.

  • Add the brown sugar to the toasted coconut.
    Stir continuously to ensure that the sugar is well distributed and melted evenly.

  • Continue cooking the mixture over medium heat until it thickens and becomes sticky. This process usually takes another 10-15 minutes.

  • To check if the bukayo is ready, take a small amount of the mixture and drop it onto a plate. If it holds its shape and doesn't spread too much, it's done.

  • If desired, add vanilla extract to enhance the flavor of the bukayo. Stir well to incorporate.

  • Once the mixture reaches the desired consistency, remove it from heat.

  • Allow it to cool slightly, so it's easier to handle.

  • While still warm, shape the bukayo into bite-sized pieces or your preferred shapes.

  • Allow the bukayo to cool completely before serving or storing. This will help it firm up.


    • ROSQUILLOS:



    Ingredients



  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ¼ cup milk


    • For the Coating:

  • ½ cup powdered sugar


    • Instructions



  • Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  • In a bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour and baking powder. Set aside.

  • In a separate large bowl, cream together the softened butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy.

  • Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.

  • Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
    If the dough is too dry, add the milk gradually until a soft dough forms.

  • Take a portion of the dough and roll it into a log shape. Cut it into 2-inch pieces and shape each piece into a round, donut-like ring.

  • Place the shaped rosquillos on the prepared baking sheet.
    Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden.

  • Allow the rosquillos to cool for a few minutes.
    While still warm, roll them in the additional powdered sugar, ensuring they are evenly coated.

  • Transfer the coated rosquillos to a wire rack to cool completely.

  • Once cooled, your delicious Cebu Rosquillos are ready to be enjoyed with a cup of coffee or tea!


    • UBE HALAYA



    Ingredients



  • 500g purple yam (ube), peeled and grated
  • 1 can (400g) condensed milk
  • 1 can (370ml) evaporated milk
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup butter or margarine
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt


    • Instructions



  • Prepare the Ube:

    a. Wash and peel the purple yam (ube).

    b. Grate the ube using a fine grater. Set aside.

  • Cooking the Ube:

    a. In a large cooking pot, combine the grated ube, condensed milk, evaporated milk, coconut milk, and sugar. .

    b. Cook over medium heat, stirring continuously to prevent sticking, until the mixture thickens.

  • Adding Butter:

    a. When the mixture has thickened, add the butter and continue stirring.

    b. Keep cooking until the mixture becomes even denser and starts to pull away from the sides of the pot.

  • Seasoning:

    - Add a pinch of salt to enhance the flavors. Adjust the sweetness according to your preference by adding more sugar if needed.

  • Continue Cooking:

    - Keep stirring to avoid burning. The mixture will become glossy and should hold its shape.

  • Molding the Ube Halaya:

    - Once the desired consistency is reached, transfer the ube halaya to a greased mold or tray. Flatten the surface using a spatula.

  • Cooling:

    - Allow the Ube Halaya to cool to room temperature before placing it in the refrigerator.

  • Chilling:

    - Refrigerate for at least 2-3 hours or overnight to set completely.

  • Serving:

    a. Once set, cut the Ube Halaya into squares or diamond shapes.

    b. Serve chilled and enjoy the rich, velvety goodness of this classic Cebuano dessert.


    • BANANA CHIPS WITH CINNAMON



    Ingredients



  • 6 ripe saba bananas (or any variety suitable for frying)
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • Vegetable oil for frying


    • Instructions



  • Prepare the Bananas:

    - Peel the bananas and thinly slice them into rounds using a mandolin or a sharp knife. Aim for consistent thickness to ensure even frying.

  • Preheat the Oil:

    - In a deep pan or fryer, heat vegetable oil to 350°F (175°C). Make sure there's enough oil to fully submerge the banana slices during frying.

  • Coat with Sugar and Cinnamon :

    - In a mixing bowl, combine the sugar and ground cinnamon. Toss the banana slices in the mixture, ensuring they are evenly coated with the cinnamon-sugar blend.

  • Fry the Banana Slices :

    - Carefully drop a few banana slices into the hot oil at a time to avoid overcrowding. Fry until the chips turn golden brown, usually for about 3-5 minutes. Use a slotted spoon to remove the chips from the oil.

  • Drain Excess Oil :

    - Place the fried banana chips on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil. Allow them to cool for a few minutes.

  • Serve and Enjoy :

    - Once the banana chips have cooled, they are ready to be enjoyed. Serve as a delightful snack or as a topping for desserts.


    • MANGO FLOAT



    Ingredients



  • 4 to 5 large ripe mangoes, peeled, pitted, and sliced thinly
  • 2 cups crushed graham crackers
  • 2 packs (250ml each) all-purpose cream, chilled
  • 1 can (300ml) condensed milk, chilled
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh milk
  • Ice water (for chilling the mixing bowl and beaters)


    • Instructions



  • Prepare the Mangoes:

    - Peel the mangoes and slice them thinly. Set aside.

  • Chill the Mixing Bowl and Beaters:

    - To ensure the cream whips properly, place the mixing bowl and beaters in the freezer for about 15-20 minutes.

  • Make the Cream Mixture:

    -In the chilled mixing bowl, combine the chilled all-purpose cream and condensed milk.

    -Add the vanilla extract.

    -Using an electric mixer, beat the mixture until it forms stiff peaks. This may take around 5-7 minutes.

  • Assemble the Mango Float:

    -In a rectangular or square glass dish, arrange a layer of graham crackers at the bottom.

    -Spoon a portion of the cream mixture on top of the graham crackers and spread it evenly.

    -Place a layer of sliced mangoes on top of the cream mixture.

    -Repeat the layers, alternating between graham crackers, cream mixture, and mango slices until you fill the dish. Ensure the top layer is the cream mixture.

  • Chill and Set:

    -Once set, slice and serve the Mango Float chilled. Enjoy the delightful layers of creamy goodness and sweet mango slices!


    • CHOCOLATE POLVORON :



    Ingredients



  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup powdered milk
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup toasted rice flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup chocolate powder (unsweetened)
  • 1/2 cup crushed nuts (optional, for added texture)
  • Polvoron molds or small cookie cutters


    • Instructions



  • Prepare the Toasted Rice Flour:

    -In a pan over medium heat, toast the rice flour until it turns light brown. Stir constantly to avoid burning. Once toasted, set it aside to cool.

  • Combine Dry Ingredients:

    -In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, powdered milk, sugar, toasted rice flour, and chocolate powder. Mix the dry ingredients thoroughly.

  • Add Melted Butter:

    -Pour the melted butter into the dry ingredients. Mix well until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Add crushed nuts if desired and mix again.

  • Shape the Polvoron:

    -Using polvoron molds or small cookie cutters, shape the mixture into round or oval-shaped molds. You can also wrap them in colorful cellophane or wax paper for a more traditional presentation.

  • Chill the Polvoron:

    -Place the shaped polvoron in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to firm up.

  • Serve and Enjoy:

    -Once chilled, your chocolate polvoron is ready to be served! Enjoy these sweet, crumbly treats with a cup of hot chocolate or your favorite beverage.


    • CEBU UBE TART :



    Ingredients



    For the Ube Filling:

  • 2 cups boiled and mashed purple yam (ube)
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cup condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract


    • For the Tart Crust:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small cubes
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup cold water
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

    For the Ube Tart Assembly:

  • Ube Filling
  • Tart Crust
  • Egg wash (1 egg beaten with a tablespoon of water)


    • Instructions



  • Prepare the Tart Crust:

    a. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, and salt.

    b. Add the cold, cubed butter to the dry ingredients.

    c. Use your fingers or a pastry cutter to incorporate the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs.

    d. Gradually add cold water and mix until the dough comes together.

    e. Form the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 unbaked pie crusts (store-bought or homemade)


    • Instructions

    • PUTO CHEESE



    Ingredients



  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup evaporated milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup grated cheese (Edam or cheddar), divided into half for batter and topping
  • Cheese slices for topping (optional)

    Instructions



  • Preparing the Batter:

    a. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

    b. In a separate bowl, whisk together the evaporated milk, water, eggs, and melted butter.

    c. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring continuously to ensure a smooth batter.

    d. Fold in half of the grated cheese into the batter.

  • Filling the Molds:

    a. Grease the puto molds or use cupcake liners to prevent sticking.

    b. Pour the batter into each mold, filling them about 3/4 full.

    b. If desired, place a small slice of cheese on top for an extra cheesy touch.

  • Steaming Process:

    a. Arrange the filled molds in a steamer basket, leaving enough space for steam circulation.

    b. Steam the puto over boiling water for about 15-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.

  • Serving:

    a. Once cooked, remove the puto from the steamer and let them cool for a few minutes.

    b. Serve warm and enjoy these delightful Puto Cheese treats!

    • YEMA

  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup crushed peanuts (optional, for coating)


    • Instructions



  • Prepare Ingredients:

    -Gather all the ingredients and ensure they are at room temperature.

    -Turn off the heat and let the mixture cool down. It will continue to thicken as it cools.

    -Once the mixture is cool enough to handle, scoop a tablespoon-sized portion and shape it into small balls using your hands.

  • Optional Coating:

    -If desired, roll the Yema balls in crushed peanuts for an added layer of flavor and texture.

  • Serve or Store:

    -Place the Yema balls in small paper cups or wrap them individually in colorful cellophane.

    -Enjoy immediately or store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for a longer shelf life.


    • MAJA BLANCA



    Ingredients



  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 can (14 ounces) condensed milk
  • 1 cup fresh milk
  • 1 cup sweet corn kernels (optional for added texture)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Grated coconut for topping


    • Instructions



  • Prepare the Mixture:

    -In a mixing bowl, dissolve the cornstarch in fresh milk. Make sure there are no lumps.

    -Add the coconut milk to the cornstarch mixture and stir well.

    -Pour the condensed milk into the bowl and mix thoroughly.

    -Add sugar and continue stirring until all ingredients are well combined.

  • Cook the Mixture:

    -Transfer the mixture to a cooking pot and cook over medium heat.

    -Continuously stir the mixture to prevent it from sticking to the bottom of the pot.

    -Once the mixture thickens, add the sweet corn kernels (if using) and vanilla extract.

    -Continue cooking and stirring until the mixture reaches a thick and smooth consistency.

  • Set and Mold:

    -Lightly grease a mold or rectangular dish with coconut oil or butter.

    -Pour the cooked mixture into the mold, spreading it evenly.

  • Cool and Slice:

    -Allow the Maja Blanca to cool and set at room temperature.

    -Once set, refrigerate for at least 2 hours or until it becomes firm.

    -When fully chilled, slice the Maja Blanca into squares or diamond shapes.

  • Serve:

    -Garnish the sliced Maja Blanca with grated coconut before serving.
