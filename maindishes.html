LECHON

a succulent whole roasted pig renowned for its crispy golden skin and tender, flavorful meat



Ingredients



  • Whole pig (approximately 20kg) - 1
  • Soy sauce - to taste
  • Salt - 600-700g
  • Pepper - 600-700g


    • For Stuffing



  • Lemongrass - 10-15 bundles
  • Bay leaves, cut into small pieces -6
  • Star anise - 60-65ml
  • Garlic, crushed - 1 1/4 kg
  • Green onion leaves - 2kg
  • (optional) Halved saba bananas, boild ro half cook - 8pcs


    • For Glazing



  • 7 cup - 1 liter (the magic ingredient)


    • Instructions



  • Start by removing the entrails of your pig and its hair (scalping).

  • Wash the pig clean with boiling water.

  • Rub salt and pepper on the meat thoroughly, and rub the insides with soy sauce.

  • Now, stuff the insides with bay leaves, star anise, garlic, green onion leaves,
    and halved saba bananas. Also, stack lemongrass right in the center of the stomach.

  • Stitch the belly carefully to ensure the juice/stuffing stays inside while cooking.

  • Next, skewer the pig on the bamboo stick and start roasting it over hot charcoal.

  • Make sure the pork belly isn’t directly beneath the hot charcoal and that you are rotating the skewer slowly.

  • Continue to glaze the pig with 7up using a sponge (to have that delightful crispy skin).

  • Cook for 3-4 hours until the meat is tender, but make sure the skin stays a nice caramel colour.

    • UTAN BISAYA

    A popular vegetable soup in Cebu, is a delectable medley of locally sourced vegetables,
    creating a nutritious and flavorful dish.



    Ingredients



  • 1 cup malunggay leaves
  • 1 cup squash, sliced
  • 1 cup string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup okra, sliced
  • 1 cup corn on the cob, sliced
  • 1 medium-sized red onion, sliced
  • 2-medium sixed tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 4 cups water
  • Salt and pepper to taste


    • Instructions



  • Slice the squash, eggplant, red onion, tomatoes, and ginger.

  • Cut the string beans and okra into 2-inch pieces.

  • Remove the malunggay leaves from the stem.

  • In a pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat.

  • Saute garlic until golden brown.

  • Add the sliced red onion and ginger. Cook until the onion becomes translucent.

  • Add the tomatoes and cook until they soften.

  • Add the squash, string beans, okra, and corn on the cob. Mix well.

  • Pour in the water and let it come to a boil.

  • Add fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. Adjust according to your preference.

  • Simmer until the vegetables are tender but not overcooked.

  • Add the eggplant and malunggay leaves. Cook for an additional 3-5 minutes
    until the eggplant is tender and the malunggay leaves are wilted.

  • Serve hot and enjoy this healthy and flavorful Utan Bisaya.

    • POCHERO

    This classic Cebuano dish is simmered to perfection with an array of vegetables like potatoes,
    saba bananas, chickpeas, green beans, and pechay, creating a harmonious blend of flavors.



    Ingredients



  • 1 kg beef shank, cut into serving pieces
  • 1/2 kg pork belly, sliced
  • 2 pieces chorizo de bilbao, sliced
  • 2 ripe saba bananas, peeled and sliced
  • 1 medium-sized cabbage, quartered
  • 1 bunch of pechay (bok choy)
  • 1 bunch of green beans, trimmed
  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and sliced
  • 2 medium-sized plantains, peeled and sliced
  • 1 large onion, peeled and sliced
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 cup chickpeas (garbanzo beans), pre-soaked and boiled until tender
  • 1 liter beef or pork broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil


    • Instructions



  • Clean and prepare all the meats and vegetables.

  • Pre-soak the chickpeas and boil until tender.

  • Slice the beef shank, pork belly, chorizo de bilbao, and vegetables.

  • In a large pot, heat cooking oil over medium heat.

  • Sauté garlic until golden brown, then add onions and cook until translucent.

  • Add the beef shank and pork belly to the pot. Brown the meats on all sides to

  • Stir in the chorizo de bilbao.

  • Mix in the tomato paste and cook for a few minutes to remove the raw taste.

  • Pour in the beef or pork broth, covering the meats completely.

  • Add bay leaves, salt, and pepper to taste

  • Bring the pot to a boil, then reduce the heat to simmer. Allow the meat to cook until tender.
    This may take 1 to 1.5 hours. Add water if necessary.

  • Once the meats are tender, add the sliced potatoes, green beans, plantains, saba bananas, and chickpeas.

  • Simmer until the vegetables are cooked but not overly soft.

    • Incorporate Leafy Vegetables:


  • Add the quartered cabbage and pechay.
    Simmer until the leafy vegetables are wilted.

  • Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning according to your preference.

  • Ladle the Pochero into bowls, making sure to include a variety of meats and vegetables in each serving.

  • Serve hot with rice and enjoy this hearty and flavorful Cebuano Pochero!

    • KINILAW NA ISDA

    a quintessential Cebuano dish,
    showcasing the region's love for fresh and bold flavors



    Ingredients



  • 500 grams fresh fish (tuna, tanigue, or lapu-lapu), deboned and cubed
  • 1 cup coconut vinegar
  • 1 cup coconut cream
  • 1 medium-sized red onion, finely sliced
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, peeled and julienned
  • 2-3 pieces red chili peppers, sliced
  • 2-3 pieces green chili peppers, sliced
  • 1 bundle fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced (for garnish)
  • 1 cup vinegar (for washing the fish)


    • Instructions



  • Rinse the fish thoroughly in vinegar to clean and eliminate any unwanted odor.

  • Pat the fish dry with a paper towel and cut it into bite-sized cubes.

  • In a bowl, combine the fish cubes, coconut vinegar, salt, and pepper.

  • Allow the fish to marinate for at least 30 minutes in the refrigerator.
    The acid from the vinegar will "cook" the fish.

  • After marinating, drain excess liquid from the fish.

  • In a separate bowl, combine the marinated fish with red onion, ginger, red and green chili peppers, and chopped cilantro.

  • Mix well to ensure even distribution of flavors.

  • Pour the coconut cream over the fish mixture.

  • Gently fold the coconut cream into the mixture until well combined.

  • Taste the kinilaw and adjust the seasoning according to your preference.
    You can add more salt, pepper, or chili peppers for extra spice.

  • Cover the bowl and refrigerate the kinilaw for at least 1 hour
    to allow the flavors to meld and enhance.

  • Before serving, garnish the kinilaw with thinly sliced cucumber for a refreshing crunch.

  • Serve chilled and enjoy the burst of flavors in this traditional Cebuano kinilaw na isda!

    • KINILAW NA ISDA



    Ingredients



  • 200g rice vermicelli noodles
  • 200g egg noodles
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 100g pork belly, thinly sliced
  • 100g chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup Chinese chorizo, sliced
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1 cup carrot, julienned
  • 1 cup snow peas, ends trimmed
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Green onions, chopped (for garnish)


    • Instructions



  • Cook rice vermicelli and egg noodles separately according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  • In a large wok or pan, heat cooking oil over medium-high heat.

  • Add minced garlic and sliced onion, sauté until fragrant.

  • Add sliced pork belly and chicken breast, cook until browned.

  • Incorporate shrimp and Chinese chorizo, cook until shrimp turns pink.

  • Toss in shredded cabbage, julienned carrot, and snow peas.

  • Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender but still crisp.

  • Add the cooked rice vermicelli and egg noodles to the wok,
    tossing them with the meat and vegetables.

  • Pour soy sauce and oyster sauce over the noodles.

  • Sprinkle sugar, salt, and pepper to taste.

  • Continuously toss and stir-fry
    until all ingredients are well-coated and heated through.

  • Adjust seasoning if necessary.

  • Garnish with chopped green onions for a burst of freshness.

  • Transfer Bam-i to a serving platter and serve hot.
    Enjoy the delightful combination of textures and flavors in this Cebuano noodle dish!

    • HUMBA



    Ingredients



  • 1 kg pork belly, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 cup pineapple juice
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon crushed garlic
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 2 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 1 cup water
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Hard-boiled eggs (optional)
  • Banana blossoms (optional)


    • Instructions



  • In a large bowl, combine pork belly, soy sauce, pineapple juice, and vinegar.

  • Add crushed garlic, black peppercorns, and dried bay leaves.

  • Marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat.

  • Heat cooking oil in a wide, heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat.

  • Pour water into the pan, ensuring that it covers the pork.

  • Bring to a boil, then lower the heat to simmer.

  • Cover and let it cook until the pork is tender, about 1 to 1.5 hours. Add water if needed.

  • Once the pork is tender, add brown sugar to the pan.

  • Stir and let it simmer until the sauce thickens and caramelizes.

  • Adjust the seasoning by adding salt or soy sauce if necessary.

  • If desired, add hard-boiled eggs and banana blossoms to the pot.

  • Cook for an additional 10-15 minutes until the flavors meld.

  • Transfer the humba to a serving dish.

  • Garnish with sliced green onions or spring onions for added freshness.

  • Serve hot with steamed rice.

    • BATCHOY



    Ingredients



  • 500 grams pork belly, sliced into thin strips
  • 300 grams pig liver, sliced into thin strips
  • 200 grams pork kidney, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 cup pork intestines, cleaned and boiled
  • 200 grams miki noodles (or any egg noodles)
  • 1 onion, minced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 8 cups beef or pork broth
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (patis)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions for garnish
  • Chicharron (pork cracklings) for topping
  • Hard-boiled eggs, sliced (optional)


    • Instructions



  • Prepare the Pork Broth:
    - In a large pot, bring the beef or pork broth to a boil.
    Add the sliced pork belly and let it simmer until tender, usually for about 30-40 minutes.
    Skim off any impurities that rise to the surface.

  • Cook the Pig Liver and Kidney:
    -In a separate pan, heat oil and sauté half of the minced garlic until golden brown.
    -Add the sliced pig liver and pork kidney. Cook until they are no longer pink. Set aside.

  • Sauté Aromatics:
    -In the same pan, add the remaining minced garlic and onions. Sauté until the onions are translucent.

  • Combine Ingredients:
    -Add the sautéed pig liver and kidney to the pot with the boiling broth and pork belly.
    -Season with fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. Let it simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes.

  • Prepare the Miki Noodles:
    -Cook the miki noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  • Assemble the Batchoy:
    -In serving bowls, place a portion of cooked miki noodles.
    -Ladle the hot broth with pork, liver, and kidney over the noodles.

  • Garnish and Serve:
    -Top with chicharron, chopped green onions, and sliced hard-boiled eggs if desired.

  • Serve the Cebu Batchoy hot and enjoy the rich and flavorful broth along with the tender meats and noodles.

    • CALAMARES



    Ingredients



  • 500g fresh squid, cleaned and sliced into rings
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp pepper
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • Cooking oil, for frying
  • Calamansi or lemon wedges, for serving


    • Instructions



  • Prepare the Squid:
    -Clean the squid thoroughly, removing the innards, cartilage, and skin.
    -Cut the squid into rings, about 1/2 inch wide.
    -Score the inner part of the squid in a crisscross pattern, being careful not to cut all the waythrough.

  • Prepare the Batter:
    -In a bowl, combine the flour, cornstarch, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Mix well.
    -Dredge the squid rings in the flour mixture, making sure they are well-coated.

  • Dip in Egg Mixture:
    -Dip the flour-coated squid rings into the beaten eggs.

  • Double Coating (Optional):
    -For a thicker and crunchier coating, you can repeat the process of coating the squid in flour and dipping in beaten eggs.

  • Frying:
    -Heat cooking oil in a deep pan or fryer to 350°F (175°C).
    -Carefully drop the squid rings into the hot oil, a few at a time, to avoid overcrowding.
    -Fry until the coating is golden brown and the squid is cooked, about 2-3 minutes per batch.
    -Use a slotted spoon to remove the calamares from the oil and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil.

  • Serve:
    -Arrange the fried calamares on a serving plate.
    -Serve hot with a side of vinegar dipping sauce or garlic mayo.
    -Garnish with calamansi or lemon wedges.

    • NGOHIONG

    a deep-fried spring roll filled with a mixture of ground pork, vegetables, and spices



    Ingredients



  • 1/2 kg ground pork
  • 1 cup ubod (heart of palm), finely julienned
  • 1/2 cup singkamas (jicama), finely julienned
  • 1/2 cup carrots, finely julienned
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup onion, minced
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp pepper
  • Lumpia wrapper (spring roll wrapper)
  • Cooking oil for frying

    • Dipping Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 red chili, chopped (optional)


    • Instructions



    FOR THE FILLING:

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine ground pork, ubod, singkamas, carrots, green onions, garlic,
    onion, flour, cornstarch, egg, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Mix thoroughly until well combined.

  • Take a lumpia wrapper and place about 2 tablespoons of the mixture in the center.

  • Fold the sides of the wrapper inward and roll the wrapper tightly to form a spring roll.

  • Heat cooking oil in a deep pan or fryer to 350°F (175°C).

  • Carefully place the ngohiong rolls into the hot oil, frying until they turn golden brown and crispy.
    Fry in batches to avoid overcrowding.

  • Once cooked, remove the ngohiong from the oil and drain excess oil by placing
    them on a plate lined with paper towels.

  • In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, vinegar, minced garlic,
    and chopped red chili (if using). Stir well to combine.

  • Serve the ngohiong hot with the dipping sauce on the side.

    • LONGGANISA



    Ingredients



  • 1 kg ground pork
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper, ground
  • 1 teaspoon red chili flakes (optional)
  • Sausage casings


    • Instructions



  • Soak the sausage casings in warm water for about 30 minutes to soften them.
    Rinse the casings thoroughly to remove excess salt.

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork,
    minced garlic, vinegar, soy sauce, sugar, salt, black pepper, and red chili flakes (if using).

  • Mix the ingredients thoroughly until well combined.
    It's a good idea to use your hands for this to ensure even distribution.

  • Using a sausage stuffer or a funnel, fill the softened casings with the pork mixture.
    Leave some space at the ends to tie knots.

  • Twist the filled casings at regular intervals to create individual longganisa links.
    Ensure they are of the desired length.

  • Hang the longganisa in a cool, dry place for about 8 to 12 hours or overnight.
    This allows the flavors to meld, and the casings to dry slightly.

  • Pan-Fry:
    - Heat a pan with a bit of oil over medium heat.
    - Pan-fry the longganisa until golden brown and cooked through.

  • Grill:
    - You can also grill the longganisa for a smokier flavor.
    - Preheat the grill and cook until well-done.

  • Serve your homemade Cebuano longganisa hot with garlic rice and vinegar on the side for dipping.

    • CHORIZO



    Ingredients



  • 1 kg ground pork
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon annatto powder (achuete)
  • Natural sausage casings


    • Instructions



  • Soak the natural sausage casings in warm water for about 30 minutes to soften them.

  • Rinse the casings thoroughly under cold running water to remove any salt.

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, vinegar, soy sauce,
    brown sugar, minced garlic, paprika, salt, black pepper, and annatto powder.

  • Mix the ingredients thoroughly, ensuring an even distribution of flavors.

  • Attach a sausage stuffer to your meat grinder.

  • Carefully thread the softened casings onto the sausage stuffer.

  • Pan-Fry:
    - Heat a pan with a bit of oil over medium heat.
    - Pan-fry the longganisa until golden brown and cooked through.

  • Stuff the casings with the chorizo mixture, being careful not to overfill.

  • Twist the chorizo into 4 to 6-inch links.

  • Tie the ends of each link with kitchen twine to secure them.

  • Hang the chorizo links in a cool, dry place to air-dry for at least 24 hours.
    This helps the flavors meld and intensify.

  • Grill the chorizo links over medium heat until cooked through, turning occasionally.
    This gives them a delicious smoky flavor.

  • Pan-Fry:
    In a skillet over medium heat, cook the chorizo links until browned on all sides and cooked through.

    • DANGGIT



    Ingredients



  • 500 grams dried danggit (rabbitfish)
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium-sized onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • Salt to taste


    • Instructions



  • Rinse the dried danggit under running water to remove excess salt.

  • Pat the danggit dry using paper towels.

  • Optional: You can lightly toast the danggit in a pan over low heat for a few minutes to enhance the flavor.

  • Heat cooking oil in a pan over medium heat.

  • Sauté minced garlic until golden brown.

  • Add thinly sliced onions and cook until softened.

  • Gently place the dried danggit in the pan, arranging them evenly.

  • Sprinkle peppercorns over the danggit for added flavor.

  • Fry the danggit for about 2-3 minutes on each side or until they turn golden brown and crispy.

  • Take care not to overcook, as danggit can become too tough if fried for too long.

  • Pour vinegar over the danggit to deglaze the pan.

  • Allow the vinegar to evaporate, leaving a tangy flavor on the danggit.

  • Season with salt to taste. Remember that danggit is naturally salty, so be cautious with the amount of additional salt.

  • Transfer the crispy danggit onto a serving plate.

  • Serve hot with garlic rice and your favorite dipping sauce, such as vinegar with crushed red pepper or atchara (pickled green papaya).
