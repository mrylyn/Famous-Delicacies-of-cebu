  • 1/2 cup Sugar
  • Banana leaves (for wrapping)

    • AMPAO

    Ingredients



  • 2 cups glutinous rice
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)


    • Instructions



  • 1. Rinse the glutinous rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
    Soak the rice in water for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  • 2. Drain the soaked rice and place it in a steamer lined with cheesecloth or muslin cloth.
    Steam the rice over medium-high heat for about 30-40 minutes or
    until the grains are fully cooked.
  • 3. While the rice is steaming, combine sugar and water in a saucepan over medium heat.
  • 4. Stir until the sugar dissolves, and the mixture becomes slightly thick. Remove from heat.
  • 5. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a large mixing bowl.
  • 6. Pour the sugar syrup over the rice and gently fold to coat the rice evenly. Let it cool for a few minutes.
  • 7. Grease your hands with vegetable oil to prevent sticking.
  • 8. Take a spoonful of the sweet rice mixture and shape it into flat,
    round discs or rectangles. You can use a mold for a more uniform shape.
  • 9.If desired, sprinkle sesame seeds on top of each ampao for added flavor and texture.
  • 10. Place the shaped ampao on a flat surface or tray lined with parchment paper.
  • 11. Allow the rice crisps to air-dry for at least 4-6 hours or until they are firm and no longer sticky.
  • 12. Once completely dry, store the ampao in an airtight
    container to maintain freshness.

    • BINGKA

    Ingredients

  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour
  • 1 cup regular rice flour
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup fresh coconut, grated
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Banana leaves for lining the pan


    • Instructions



  • Cut banana leaves into squares and lightly pass them over an open flame to make them pliable.
    Line your baking pan with the banana leaves.
  • In a large mixing bowl, combine glutinous
    rice flour, regular rice flour, and salt. Mix well.
  • In a separate bowl, mix coconut milk, sugar, grated coconut, and water.
    Stir until the sugar is dissolved.
  • Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients.
    Mix well until the batter becomes smooth and lump-free.
  • Pour the mixture into the prepared baking pan lined with banana leaves.
  • Steam the mixture for about 30-45 minutes or until a
    toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  • Make sure to cover the pan with a clean cloth before placing
    the lid to prevent water from dripping onto the bingka.
  • Allow the bingka to cool completely before slicing.
    Serve and enjoy the delicious Cebuano rice cake!

    • BUD-BUD BALANGHOY

    Ingredients



  • 2 cups grated cassava
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cup coconut cream
  • 1 cup sugar
  • Banana leaves, cleaned and cut into rectangles (for wrapping)
  • A pinch of salt




    • Instructions

  • Pass banana leaves over an open flame to make them pliable.
    This helps prevent the leaves from tearing during wrapping.
  • Combine grated cassava,
    coconut milk, coconut cream, sugar, and a pinch of salt in a bowl.
  • Mix the ingredients thoroughly until well combined.
  • Place a portion of the cassava mixture in the center of a banana leaf.
  • Fold the sides of the banana leaf over the mixture, then fold the top and bottom to form a rectangular packet. Secure with toothpicks or kitchen twine.
  • Arrange the wrapped budbud balanghoy in a steamer.
  • Steam over medium heat for about 45 minutes
    to 1 hour or until the cassava is fully cooked.
  • The budbud is done when it becomes firm and no longer sticky.
  • Allow the budbud balanghoy to cool slightly before serving.
  • Unwrap the banana leaves and slice the budbud into serving portions.
  • Serve with freshly grated coconut on top for added flavor.
  • Budbud balanghoy pairs well with hot chocolate or coffee.

  • ½ cup powdered sugar


    • Instructions

    • DRIED MANGO

    Ingredients



  • 2 cups ripe mangoes, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup water




    • Instructions



  • Peel and thinly slice them into uniform pieces.
  • In a saucepan, combine 1 cup of water and 1 cup of granulated sugar.
  • Stir over medium heat until the sugar completely dissolves, creating a simple syrup.
  • Place the sliced mangoes in a bowl.
  • Pour the prepared sugar syrup over the mango slices, ensuring they are fully submerged.
  • Let the mango slices soak in the syrup for about 30 minutes to allow them to absorb the sweetness.
  • After soaking, drain the mango slices, removing excess syrup.
  • Arrange the mango slices on a wire rack or a lined baking sheet, ensuring they are spaced apart.
  • Place the arranged mango slices in direct sunlight.
  • Allow them to dry for 2 to 3 days, turning the slices occasionally for even drying.
  • Bring the mangoes indoors during the night to prevent moisture absorption.
  • If you have a food dehydrator, place the mango slices on the trays.
  • Follow the dehydrator's instructions for drying fruit, typically around 135°F (57°C) for 8-12 hours.
  • The dried mangoes should be leathery but slightly pliable. They should not be sticky.
  • Allow the dried mango slices to cool completely.
  • Store them in an airtight container or sealable bags to maintain freshness.

    • BINIGNIT

    Ingredients



  • 1 cup glutinous rice (malagkit)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 cup ripe langka (jackfruit), sliced
  • 2 cups sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 cups taro (gabi), peeled and cubed
  • 2 cups ripe saba bananas, sliced
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup small tapioca pearls (sago)
  • 1/2 cup yellow split mung beans (monggo), peeled and boiled
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt


    • Instructions



  • Rinse the glutinous rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
  • In a pot, combine the glutinous rice and 4 cups of water.
  • Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer until the rice is cooked and has absorbed most of the water.
  • Boil the small tapioca pearls (sago) according to package instructions.
  • Once cooked, drain and set aside.
  • Peel and boil the yellow split mung beans (monggo) until tender.Drain and set aside.
  • In a separate pot, combine the coconut milk, sugar, and a pinch of salt.
  • Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat, stirring until the sugar is completely dissolved.
  • Add the vanilla extract and mix well. Set aside.
  • Add the langka, sweet potatoes, taro, and ripe saba bananas to the pot with the cooked glutinous rice.
  • Pour in the coconut milk mixture and stir well.
  • Allow the mixture to simmer until the sweet potatoes and taro are tender.
  • Incorporate the cooked tapioca pearls and mung beans into the pot, stirring gently to combine.
  • Simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes until all the ingredients are well-incorporated and heated through.
  • Ladle the binignit into bowls.
